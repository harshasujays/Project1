Kerala

God’s Own Country

Kerala, located on the southwestern coast of India, is one of the most beautiful and peaceful states in the country. Popularly known as “God’s Own Country”, Kerala is famous for its lush green landscapes, serene backwaters, palm-lined beaches, misty hill stations, and rich cultural heritage. It is a paradise for nature lovers, honeymoon couples, families, and wellness travelers.

Historical Significance

Kerala has a long and fascinating history that dates back thousands of years. It was an important center of spice trade and attracted traders from Arabia, China, and Europe. The region was ruled by ancient dynasties like the Cheras and later came under the influence of Portuguese, Dutch, and British colonial powers. This blend of cultures has shaped Kerala’s unique traditions, architecture, and religious harmony.

Important Places to Visit

  • Munnar: Famous for tea plantations, cool climate, waterfalls, and scenic viewpoints.
  • Alleppey (Alappuzha): Known for its beautiful backwaters and houseboat cruises.
  • Kochi: Historic port city with Chinese fishing nets, forts, and colonial architecture.
  • Wayanad: Hill district with forests, wildlife sanctuaries, caves, and waterfalls.
  • Varkala & Kovalam: Popular beach destinations with stunning cliff and sea views.
  • Thekkady: Home to Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary and boat safaris.

Culture & Festivals

  • Onam: The most famous harvest festival celebrated with flower decorations, snake boat races, and traditional dances.
  • Vishu: Kerala New Year festival marked by special rituals and celebrations.
  • Kathakali & Mohiniyattam: Traditional dance forms that reflect Kerala’s artistic heritage.
  • Local Cuisine: Try Sadya, Appam and Stew, Puttu and Kadala Curry, and fresh seafood cooked with coconut and spices.

Travel Tips

  • Best time to visit: October to March for pleasant weather and sightseeing.
  • Carry light cotton clothes and a light jacket for hill stations.
  • Book houseboats and resorts in advance during peak season.
  • Respect local customs and dress modestly at temples and religious places.
  • Do not miss Ayurvedic treatments and village life experiences.
