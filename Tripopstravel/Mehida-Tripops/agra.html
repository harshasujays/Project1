Agra

The City of the Taj

Agra, located in Uttar Pradesh, is one of India’s most famous cities, best known for the magnificent Taj Mahal, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the Seven Wonders of the World. The city has a rich Mughal heritage, with beautiful forts, palaces, and gardens. Agra is a hub for history lovers and photography enthusiasts.

Historical Significance

Agra served as the capital of the Mughal Empire under emperors like Akbar, Jahangir, and Shah Jahan. Key historical monuments include Agra Fort, Fatehpur Sikri, and the Taj Mahal. The city played an important role in India’s history, blending Persian and Indian architectural styles.

Important Places to Visit

  • Taj Mahal: Iconic white marble mausoleum built by Shah Jahan for Mumtaz Mahal.
  • Agra Fort: Massive red sandstone fort with palaces, mosques, and halls.
  • Fatehpur Sikri: Abandoned Mughal city with stunning palaces and mosques.
  • Itmad-ud-Daulah’s Tomb: Known as the "Baby Taj," famous for intricate marble inlay work.
  • Mehtab Bagh: Garden across the Yamuna river with perfect views of Taj Mahal.

Culture & Festivals

  • Urs Festival at Taj Mahal: Celebrated in honor of Mumtaz Mahal’s death anniversary.
  • Diwali & Holi: Celebrated with lights, colors, and traditional festivities.
  • Local Cuisine: Try petha (sweet), Mughlai dishes, and street snacks.

Travel Tips

  • Best time to visit: October to March for pleasant weather.
  • Start early for Taj Mahal to avoid crowds and heat.
  • Hire a local guide for historical insights at Agra Fort and Fatehpur Sikri.
  • Use auto-rickshaws, cabs, or cycle rickshaws for local travel.
  • Plan 1–2 days to cover main attractions comfortably.
